About the author
Charles Dickens (1812–1870), famed English novelist, exposed social injustices through works like Oliver Twist, blending vivid characters with sharp social critique.
Overview
Oliver Twist follows a poor orphan through workhouses, crime-ridden London, and encounters with Fagin, the Artful Dodger, and the dangerous Bill Sikes. Amid betrayal and fleeting kindness, Oliver's fate hangs uncertain—will innocence survive a world of corruption?
